A Medic's Job[]
Laurence Wayne, Rosetta Hospital Director
Dayton Hospital, North Dayton Wetlands
March 2, ▮▮19
As I told you last time, we must be extremely careful when filling out medical forms. We must take care to avoid any language that could be prejudicial.
For example, "crazy, aggressive behavior" could be rephrased as "physical agitation brought on by neurological symptoms"; "patient broke free of restrains even after tripe dose of sedation" might be better worded as "patient did not respond as expected to routine medication." "Dr. Henry Winsor died after sustaining serious injuries" could be changed to something like "Dr. Winsor has since left medical practice." Do you see what I mean?
And I don't want to hear any more of your wild gossip either. You and I are just tiny specks of dust in the 7th Rosetta Medical Division. I'm sure you're aware that over at the Containment Division, there are occasionally some... unnatural accidents. But they always manage to keep these accidents under control, right? if they hadn't, the whole world would've been destroyed.
It's our job to do our best to treat the patients they bring us. If the patients can't be saved, we follow official procedures and dispose of the bodies. That's it.
